TENSE PAIRS

present Simple/Present Continue

(a) I live in Beijing. present Simple
(b) I am living in Beijing. present Continue

一般现在时：永久情况；一般进行时：暂态。一般现在时一般用于在科技论文写作中用以陈述公理或者一般事实。

==在陈述自己的结果时==，也可以使用一般现在时而不是用一般过去时，用以表示自己相信自己的基本概念和推论足够有力，足以被视为事实或真理。

(a) We found that the pressure increased as the temperature rose*, which indicated that temperature played a significant role in the process.

(b) We found that the pressure increases as the temperature rises, which indicates that temperature plays a significant role in the process.

Past Simple/Present Perfect

衍生含义
(a)Past Simple: I lived in Tokyo for 5 years. but I don’t live there anymore.
(b) Present Perfect: I have lived in Tokyo for five years… and I still live there NOW.
(c) Past Simple: I broke my glasses… but it doesn’t matter/I repaired them.
(d) Present Perfect: I have broken my glasses… and so I can’t see properly NOW.

不同的是对现在的影响是否还在，动作|描述现在是否还存续

SIGNALLING LANGUAGE

sentence connection

（1）overlap 重叠，重复前一个句子的内容。

The pattern of inflammation during an asthma attack is different from that seen in stable asthma. In stable asthma the total number of inflammatory cells does not increase.

One way to toughen polymers is to incorporate a layer of rubber particles. As a result, there has been extensive research regarding the rubber modification of PLA.

（2）use a pronoun (it,they) or pro-form (this method, these systems) 指代词

Many researchers have suggested ways of reducing cost without affecting the quality of the image. These methods rely on data structures built during a preprocessing step

（3）semicolon or relative clause（a ‘which’ clause）分号或者which从句

当两个句子非常接近，其中一个句子非常短时，用分号将句子连接起来效果很好。

Th e procedure for testing whether components are operationally safe usually takes many hours ； this means that tests are rarely repeated.

It has received much attention over the past few decades due to its biodegradable properties, which offer important economic benefits.

（4）signalling sentence connector 句子连接词

==CAUSE==

后加n. 后加句子/n.
due to (the fact that) as
on account of (the fact that) because
in view of (the fact that) since

  • Be careful when you use since; it is also often used to mean ‘from that time’, so if there’s any possibility of confusion, choose a different connector.

  • All these connectors can be used at the start of a sentence, even because(Because the measuring instruments were inaccurate, the experiment was unsuccessful).

==RESULT==
therefore as a result (of which)
consequently which is why
hence so

不能用So做句子开头，不正式

==CONTRAST/DIFFERENCE== 不同

British students are all vegetarians, __ Norwegian students eat meat every day.
however on the other hand
whereas while
but by contrast

on the contrary 和 conversely不适用于以上句子，因为它们不仅交流差异；它们传达了“完全相反是真的”的事实（因为素食者和肉食者不是对立的，它们只是不同的）应该用于以下情况。

Some experiments used uncalibrated instruments and succeeded; conversely*, other experiments used carefully calibrated instruments and failed.*

while使用时也需要注意，因为他经常被用于表示”at the/that same time“

==UNEXPECTEDNESS== 意外的

(a) _ it was difficult, a solution was eventually found.

(b) _ the difficulty, a solution was eventually found.

(c) It was difficult; __ a solution was eventually found.

用于句头，后接（意外对象）句子 用于句头，后接（意外对象）名词 后接（转折对象）句子
(a) Although (b) Despite (c) nevertheless
(a) Even though (b) In spite of (c) however
(a) Though (b) Regardless of (c) yet
(b) Notwithstanding (c) nonetheless
(c) even so

still 和 anyway 是不正式的用法

==ADITTION==

We used a batch processing system because it was more effective; _ it was faster.
in addition also
moreover secondly (etc.)
furthermore in the second place (etc.)
apart from that/which what is more

beside it’s more ==powerful== and is therefore better used in more persuasive（更有说服力的） contexts.

Passive/Active （被动主动）

You can use we to refer to your research group or team, but do not use it to refer to people or humanity in general.

If you are referring to ==people in general==, it’s better to use a construction with It (It is known/ thought that…) rather than We know/think that… It is also common to use the passive instead of we, especially in the central ‘report’ section (was measured, was added, etc.).

Since you cannot write your thesis using I, you will probably write in the passive. Use words like here and in this study to let your reader know when you are referring to your own work.

Use a ‘dummy’(虚拟) subject to take the place of I or we：

|This article describes an algorithm for clustering sequences into index classes.|

|The present paper presents a set of criteria for selecting such a component.|

在科技论文中大量使用被动和虚拟代词，特别是Introduction。有时候使用被动会让读者不知道这个结果的工作是由谁产生的，所以这个时候需要使用虚拟代词。

==PARAGRAPHING==

为什么分段重要？

段落是有效阅读和写作的重要视觉辅助工具。分段的两个常见错误是短或单句段落，以及段落太长。这两个错误都会让读者感到困惑，而且是写作组织不善的迹象。

What is the connection between bad and good paragraphing?

Your aim when you skim through a text is to find out quickly what it is about and where the various pieces of information are located so that you can read it faster and more confidently

略读技巧：标题-作者的名字-检查日期-读摘要-读第一段-读每段第一句话-读图表-读最后一段

所以一般略读的时候都会快速查看==每段中的第一句话==。在学术写作中，一个段落通常以一个主题句子开始，它给出了这个段落的主要思想，并告诉读者这一段落是关于什么的。

当你计划你的论文时，写下每个你想谈论的每个想法/概念，检查它们是否按照逻辑顺序，然后用要点列出你想对每个想法说什么。这将帮助您创建具有逻辑和连贯结构的段落。

1.3 Writing Task: Build a Model

Building a model (仿写教程)

One way to fi nd out what the writer is doing in a sentence — rather than what s/he is saying — is to imagine that your computer has accidentally deleted it.

另一种弄清楚作者在句子中做什么的方法是观察语法和词汇线索。主动词的时态是什么？这个时态通常用来什么？它和前一句话是一样的时态吗？如果没有，为什么作者改变了时态？作者选择使用什么词？

大多数研究文章==首先表明该研究领域或主题是有用的或重要的==。他们可能会关注这一领域的研究数量，或者在这一领域的研究有多有用，或者仅仅是这个研究领域有多重要。如果你看一下你的目标文章，你可能会在前一两句话中找到一些确定研究意义的东西。像much study in recent years or plays a major role 这样的短语在这里很常见。

What if I don’t have the confidence to say that my research is important?（如果我觉得我做的东西很水）相信自己。

该用什么时态：像近年来或过去五年的许多研究这样的短语之后通常是现在完成时（Much study in recent years has focused on…）。其他建立意义的方法可以使用一般现在时（Th ere are substantial benefi ts to be gained from…）。

Model
1 ESTABLISH THE IMPORTANCE OF YOUR FIELD
PROVIDE BACKGROUND FACTS/INFORMATION (possibly from research)
DEFINE THE TERMINOLOGY IN THE TITLE/KEY WORDS
PRESENT THE PROBLEM AREA/CURRENT RESEARCH FOCUS
2 PREVIOUS AND/OR CURRENT RESEARCH AND CONTRIBUTIONS
3 LOCATE A GAP IN THE RESEARCH
DESCRIBE THE PROBLEM YOU WILL ADDRESS
PRESENT A PREDICTION TO BE TESTED
4 DESCRIBE THE PRESENT PAPER
